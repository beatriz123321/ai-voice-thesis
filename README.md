# The Effect of a Smiling Artificial Voice on Trust
### The Roles of Warmth and Realism
 
Master's thesis submitted in partial fulfillment of the degree of Master of Science, Master Information Studies (Information Systems track), Faculty of Science, University of Amsterdam.
 

## Overview
 
This thesis investigates how smiling prosody in an artificial voice affects users' perceptions of warmth, realism, and trust during a voice-based AI interaction. It uses a two-stage quantitative design built around a fictional AI travel assistant, "John":
 
1. **Study 1 — Voice Preference Survey (N = 17):** Participants rated and ranked eight ElevenLabs-generated voices (four female, four male) to select the most suitable voice for the main experiment.
2. **Study 2 — Voice Interaction and Perception Survey (N = 39):** Using the selected voice, participants completed a website-based travel-planning interaction under two conditions — a neutral voice and a smiling voice — and rated their perceptions using subscales of the Voice Experience Inventory (VOXI).
**Research question:**
How does smiling prosody in an artificial voice influence users' perceptions of warmth, realism, and trust in the AI system?
 
**Hypotheses:**
- **H1:** A smiling voice will be perceived as warmer than a neutral voice.
- **H2:** A smiling voice will be perceived as more realistic than a neutral voice.
- **H3:** A smiling voice will be perceived as more trustworthy than a neutral voice.
**Keywords:** Artificial Voices, Trust, Realism, Warmth, Human-Computer Interaction, TTS, Pitch
