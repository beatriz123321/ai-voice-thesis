# The Effect of a Smiling Artificial Voice on Trust
### The Roles of Warmth and Realism
 
Master's thesis submitted in partial fulfillment of the degree of Master of Science, Master Information Studies (Information Systems track), Faculty of Science, University of Amsterdam.
 

## Overview
 
This thesis investigates how an expressive voice in an artificial voice affects users' perceptions of comfort, anthropomorphism, and trustworthiness during a voice-based AI interaction. It uses a two-stage quantitative design built around a fictional AI travel assistant, "John":
 
1. **Study 1 — Voice Preference Survey (N = 17):** Participants rated and ranked eight ElevenLabs-generated voices (four female, four male) to select the most suitable voice for the main experiment.
2. **Study 2 — Voice Interaction and Perception Survey (N = 48):** Using the selected voice, participants completed a website-based travel-planning interaction under two conditions — a neutral voice and a smiling voice — and rated their perceptions using subscales of the Voice Experience Inventory (VOXI).

**Research question:**
How does vocal expressiveness in an artificial voice influence users’ perceptions of comfort, anthropomorphism, and trustworthiness in an AI system?
 
**Hypotheses:**
- **H1:** An expressive voice will score higher on perceived comfort than a neutral voice.
- **H2:** An expressive voice will score higher on perceived anthropomorphism than a neutral voice.
- **H3:** An expressive voice will score higher on perceived trustworthiness than a neutral voice.
